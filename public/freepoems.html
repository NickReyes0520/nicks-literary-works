PILIPINAS: SA MGA PULO NG DAGAT

Author: Ronie “Nick” G. Reyes

Date Created: May 20, 2020

    Sa pagsikat ng araw’y unang sinisinagan,
    Pitong libo niyang pulo sa Malayong Silangan.
    Dalisay at luntian tuwing liliwanagan—
    Lupain ng pag-ibig, pag-asa't katapangan.

    Sa Dagat Pasipiko’y hiyas ang kapuluan;
    Mula Luzon, Visayas, at Mindanao ay nariyan.
    Watawat niya’y dakila sa diwang makabayan,
    Maging dagat niya’t bundok, hanggang sa kapatagan.

    Lupang pinagdanakan ng dugo ng magiting,
    At ng mga bayaning ‘di na natin kapiling.
    Liglig sa kayumihan at pag-asang kasiping,
    Hitik sa yamang likas at makulay na sining.

    Perlas ng Silanganan, kung ito ay tawagin—
    Kariktan niya’y salamin na mahirap basagin.
    Tila ba kabundukan na ‘di kayang patagin,
    Ngunit mga dayuha’y nagawa siyang bihagin.

    Nanangis sa hilahil ang bayan kong minahal;
    Inalipin sa dusa’t niyurakan ang dangal.
    Pananakop at hirap ay lalo pang tumagal,
    Bawat bibig ay tikom, waring may mga busal.

    Hanggang mga bayani ay natutong lumaban,
    At baguhin ang palad ng kawawa kong bayan.
    Mula kay Lapu-Lapu, na nagwagi sa Mactan—
    Dayuhan ay nalipol; pag-asa ay nahagkan.

    Nang ang mga Kastila’y lubusang pumarito,
    Ginamit ni Rizal ang natatanging talino.
    Sa Noli Me Tangere’t El Filibusterismo,
    Kaaway ay nilupig na parang ipo-ipo.

    At nang siya ay pumanaw, si Bonifacio’y nariyan;
    Mapayapang labana’y kaniya nang tinuldukan.
    Bilang tagatatag at ama ng Katipunan,
    Kalaban niya’y ginupo alang-alang sa bayan.

    At nang mga Kastila’y unti-unting natalo,
    Bansa’y ‘pinagbili sa mga Amerikano.
    Panibagong banyaga at bagong mga tao—
    Bagong mang-aalipin at bagong mga amo.

    Sa muling pagdirigma, gaya ng sinauna,
    May isang heneral na tumindig at nanguna.
    Kilala sa tapang, at bayan ang inuuna:
    Nag-iisang mabangis na si Antonio Luna.

    At maging sa paglipas ng marami pang taon,
    Ang bansang Pilipinas ay sinakop ng Hapon.
    Digmaan ay sumiklab, tila alab ng dragon;
    Bayan ay naalipin sa mahabang panahon.

    Bagama’t lumipas na’ng maraming mga araw,
    At ang mga bayani’y malaon nang pumanaw,
    Sa’ting mga gunita’y lagi nawang dumalaw—
    Ang diwang Pilipinong walang makaaagaw.

    Ngunit ang Pilipinas ay sadyang pinagpala:
    Laya’t kapayapaan ngayo’y tinatamasa.
    Laging may gabay ng Dios na hindi nagsasawa,
    Na maiwawangis sa ‘di natutuyong sapa.

    Sa bisa ng hula sa Banal na Kasulatan,
    Panukala ng Dios ay nagkaro’ng katuparan.
    Lingkod Niya ay nagmula sa pinili Niyang bayan—
    Gawain Niya’y lumitaw sa Malayong Silangan.

    Sa kabutihan ng Dios ay Kaniyang minarapat
    Na bayan ay tumindig sa maraming habagat.
    Mga pulo at tubig na Kaniyang ‘pinaglapat:
    Bansang Pilipinas, sa mga pulo ng dagat.

WE ALL MOVE JUST LIKE CHESS PIECES

Author: Ronie “Nick” G. Reyes

Date Created: March 15, 2020

    Each soul on earth is placed with care and firm design,
    No role too small, no crown too great, no greater sign.
    A pawn may rise through trials fierce, beyond the board,
    And reach the rank of king or queen with God's accord.

    The rook stands firm, a fortress at the battle's side,
    While knights move strange, but still in grace they do abide.
    The bishop leans with purpose on its slanted way,
    And every move is part of what the Lord would say.

    We envy those whose path seems brighter than our own,
    But fail to see how deeply their seeds had been sown.
    The king may wear the weight of war upon his head,
    While pawns in peace may find the softer path instead.

    Comparison is theft of joy, a silent snare,
    It blinds our eyes from knowing how much we all bear.
    Each soul was crafted by a Master’s perfect hand,
    A piece placed right where only it was meant to stand.

    Some move with speed and gain the glory in one dash,
    While others crawl through fire, rain, and bitter ash.
    But still they move, and that’s what matters in the end—
    Their road may twist, but it was shaped by God to bend.

    The board is wide, the night is cold, the game is long,
    Yet still we play, and still we sing our sacred song.
    Not all are made for shining light or winning praise—
    Some light the stars unseen through dark and winding days.

    Let not your heart grow weary when your turn is slow,
    For every pause contains a place you need to grow.
    And if you feel forgotten in the heat of play,
    Know God sees all and leads your soul a better way.

    The Grandmaster, unseen, commands with holy eyes,
    He knows the fall, the rise, the hows, the whens, the whys.
    No move is made that lies beyond His grand intent,
    For even loss becomes a sacred instrument.

    A queen may fall so justice learns to stand again,
    A pawn may die to spare a brother soul from pain.
    The knight may leap through chaos just to guard the weak,
    The bishop prays where none can hear the words they speak.

    The world is cruel, the tempter’s voice is sharp and sly,
    But God is near, His wisdom reaching from on high.
    Though trials press and life may pin you to a wall,
    No piece is lost who answers to the Master's call.

    So move with grace, and never doubt the path you're in,
    For He who guides you also knows how you will win.
    The crown you seek is not of gold or fleeting fame—
    It’s peace that stays and joy that glorifies His name.

    In every check, in every fall, in every fight,
    You are still His, and you’re moving toward the Light.
    Let not the world define your worth or steal your peace—
    We all move just like chess pieces, meant to cease.
